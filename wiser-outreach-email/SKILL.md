# WISER Email Writer

Use this skill for WISER ASIA work emails, especially English business-development emails to European medtech, healthtech, deep-tech, AI healthcare, screening-device, elderly-care, OEM, and investment contacts.

Default response language to Fann is Chinese. The email draft itself is usually English unless Fann asks otherwise.

## First Recover Context

Before drafting, use available context in this order:

1. Current user-provided email/thread/company materials.
2. Workspace memory: `.agents/memory/work-learning/START_HERE.md` and `facts.md`; read `evidence-log.md` when prior people, companies, screenshots, or threads are referenced.
3. Any current local file path Fann names.

Do not ask Fann to repeat context that is already in memory or in the current thread.

For a reply or follow-up, extract a compact fact block first:

- Recipient and relationship status.
- Product facts: what it is, target users, clinical/industrial use, AI/cloud/software components, certifications, traction.
- Recipient's latest stated need: distribution, China entry, licensing, JV, financing, local manufacturing, investors, clinical pilots, or meeting.
- Existing China/Asia footprint: team, sales office, partner, Hainan/Hong Kong/mainland status, pilots, regulatory state.
- WISER's relevant leverage: Optomed/AURORA, primary-care/community-hospital screening, AI-assisted reporting, cloud data, hospitals, OEMs, investors, China/Asia operations.
- Exact next step needed: answer questions, propose call, send deck, introduce person, confirm slots, or follow up gently.

## Choose The Voice

Use one voice unless Fann asks for variants.

- **Fann voice**: direct, fast, specific, good for efficient outreach or practical follow-up.
- **Judy voice**: warmer and more narrative, good when trust-building or policy/ecosystem framing matters.
- **Kevin voice**: CEO-level value-creation framing, good for founders, CEOs, chairs, listed-company executives, financing/investor angles, OEM/industrial ecosystem angles, or when the email should sound senior and commercially forceful.

## Core WISER Positioning

WISER is not just a distributor. Frame WISER as a China/Asia market-entry partner and venture developer that can help create real commercial traction through:

- China market entry and localization strategy.
- Primary-care, community-hospital, elderly-care, chronic-disease, and screening workflows.
- AI-assisted analysis/reporting, cloud data management, and referral/follow-up pathways.
- Hospital, OEM, channel, industry ecosystem, and investor access.
- Practical execution: pilot, technical evaluation, qualification, design-in, flagship customers, and financing or JV support when relevant.

Use concrete references when relevant: Optomed JV / AURORA eye screening; portable screening devices; Chinese AI/reporting partners; community hospital and primary-care deployment logic. Do not exaggerate or invent commitments.

## Fann Voice

Best for concise BD emails where the company is already understood.

Structure:

1. Specific attention: "We've been following [Company]..." plus one precise proof point.
2. WISER positioning: China/Europe market-entry partner plus one concrete reference.
3. China opportunity: market data, policy, customer need, or named scenario.
4. Commercial questions: pricing, bundling, licensing, China strategy, registry/NMPA, partner needs.
5. Clear next step: Teams/Zoom call or specific response.

Tone: professional, data-backed, direct. Specific facts beat general enthusiasm.

## Judy Voice

Best when the recipient needs more trust and background.

Structure:

1. Warm opening and identity.
2. Show product understanding: not simply what the product is, but what workflow or ecosystem it enables.
3. China rationale: clinical need/data, policy alignment, and WISER project synergy.
4. Ask whether China/Asia is a market they are considering.
5. Low-friction intro call.

Tone: formal but warm, explanatory, ecosystem-aware.

## Kevin Voice

Best for senior commercial outreach where the goal is to make WISER feel like a value-creation partner, not a service vendor.

Structure:

1. Senior identity and WISER thesis:
   "I'm Kevin Chen, CEO of WISER ASIA in China. We help Nordic/European technology companies build real commercial traction in China by connecting them with local industry networks, customers, and investors."
2. Recipient-specific proof point:
   Mention a recent milestone, profitable growth focus, OEM win, regulatory traction, listed-company status, or product-market proof.
3. China window:
   Translate that proof point into a 12-24 month China opportunity, such as 2-3 flagship OEM/customer programs, anchor hospitals, technical evaluation, qualification, design-in, or primary-care/community-hospital rollout.
4. WISER leverage:
   Name the ecosystem door WISER can open: top-tier industrial OEMs, selected hospitals, channel partners, China/Asia operators, investors, or portfolio/JV partners.
5. Value creation:
   Explain why China traction matters to the recipient: investor signal, next funding round, valuation, shareholder value, strategic/financial investors, higher-margin business, or go-global leverage.
6. Respect existing footprint:
   If they already have China staff or distributors, say WISER complements them by opening C-level and ecosystem doors that are hard to access from a pure sales perspective.
7. Close:
   Ask for a brief Teams call and invite time slots.

Reusable Kevin-style moves:

- Turn a product milestone into a China adoption thesis.
- Use "2-3 flagship/anchor programs" instead of vague market entry.
- Link WISER resources to measurable commercial wins.
- Frame China traction as useful both commercially and for capital markets.
- Offer "enhanced operations" or ecosystem leverage, not replacement of the company's team.

Keep Kevin voice strong but clean. Fix grammar and remove overlong sentences while preserving the strategic thrust.

## Follow-Up Rhythm

- Reply to the latest inbound question before pitching a call.
- Preserve `Re:` for existing threads unless Fann asks for a new subject.
- Start formally with `Mr./Ms. + surname` when appropriate; switch to first name after the other side does.
- Acknowledge illness, travel, holidays, or delays warmly and reduce pressure.
- When internally forwarding or looping in Kevin/Judy, keep the note short and action-oriented.
- If meeting scheduling is the only task, do not reopen the whole WISER pitch.

## Drafting Rules

- Subject lines should be specific: company + China/Asia opportunity, JV, pilot, OEM, screening, or meeting.
- Open with evidence that the company was studied.
- Use exact numbers only when they are known or sourceable; otherwise state the need qualitatively.
- Ask concrete commercial questions instead of broad "cooperation" questions.
- Prefer one narrow entry wedge over a broad list of possibilities.
- Do not include confidentiality notices or full signatures unless Fann asks or provides them.
- Do not store or reproduce raw private email threads in the skill; use distilled patterns only.

## Quality Check

Before returning the draft, verify:

- Company, product, person names, and role are correct.
- The draft is clearly cold outreach, warm follow-up, internal note, or scheduling email.
- The first two paragraphs prove specific knowledge of the recipient.
- WISER's value is concrete: customers, hospitals, OEMs, investors, pilots, regulatory/commercial pathway, or ecosystem access.
- The China angle is actionable and narrow.
- The final ask is specific.
- No invented claims, exaggerated certainty, or generic "China is huge" language.
